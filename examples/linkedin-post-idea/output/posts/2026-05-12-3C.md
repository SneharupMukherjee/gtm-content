I've noticed that coaches with 10 or more clients all run into the same gap. When a client goes dark, there's no protocol. Every disappearance becomes its own small crisis:

Do I follow up or does that feel like pressure?
Did they not like the work, or is life just happening?
Have I done too little, or am I about to do too much?

One freelancer in a thread this week described it exactly: she'd done the work, sent the ideas, held up her end. Then her client mentioned a family crisis and went quiet. Months later, still nothing. No approval, no pause, no "I'll be back." Just limbo.

She wasn't asking about the $1500. She was asking how to feel okay about sending a second message.

That's the gap. Not the ghosting — coaches get ghosted. It's that without a clear re-engagement process, every quiet client becomes a judgment call about whether you're being professional or cold or too much.

The ones who've figured this out don't agonize. They have a line they send on day 14. Same every time.
